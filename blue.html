Aerotéc – BLUE Turbojet Project

BLUE is a student-led initiative focused on the development of small-scale turbojet engines. Within the design team, I was assigned responsibility for the compressor stage — specifically the radial impeller and diffuser.

My work involved modelling turbomachinery behaviour in MATLAB to dimension key engine components and determine performance parameters. These analytical models were used to guide aerodynamic optimization and mechanical feasibility.

Using CAD tools, I designed the radial impeller and diffuser geometry, integrating aerodynamics principles, system-level constraints, and manufacturability considerations. The goal was to contribute to overall propulsion efficiency and structural reliability.
